This experiement allows you to create sounds from oscillators!

Pick a waveform and observe how it sounds like to your ears. Adjust the frequency to observe changes. Adjust detune to observe how a fundamental frequency can be tweaked slightly to sound different. Adjust the gain if it gets too loud.

For those with a little bit of knowledge on music, check out how detuning at specific intervals affects the sound. Remember, 1200 cents an octave!

Significance

As a basic signal source that we employ widely in EEE (your lab function generators!), oscillators are the building block of sound.

Oscillators generate basic functions with varying parameters (frequency, amplitude etc) to synthesise music for our ears. Recall from your lecture that a complex signal can be reduced to the superposition of many basic waves.
